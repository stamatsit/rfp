## Proprietary Research Studies

### TeensTALK (20+ years)
Annual study of college-bound high school students, conducted in partnership with Chegg (reaching 15+ million students) and ACT. Key findings:
- **~33%** of students research at least **10 colleges** before applying
- **40%** plan to enroll within **30 miles** of home
- Average distance: just over **200 miles** from home
- Students increasingly begin serious searches in **junior year** (shift from senior year)
- "Offers my intended major" is the **#1 factor** in adding a college to consideration

> *Sources: [Inside Higher Ed: Survey on What College Applicants Want](https://www.insidehighered.com/quicktakes/2016/11/07/survey-what-college-applicants-want); [PR Newswire: TeensTALK 2012](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/home-is-where-the-heart-is-152717045.html); [ACT Conference PDF](https://www.act.org/content/dam/act/unsecured/documents/20160714_T3.4-2016-TeensTALK-Study.pdf)*

### AdultStudentsTALK
Annual study on recruiting and supporting adult learners. Key findings (2019):
- **91%** of admissions professionals rate adult undergraduate recruitment as "moderately" to "very" important
- **96.3%** rate graduate student recruitment as "moderately" to "very" important
- **63%** of admissions marketers **lack** a dedicated recruitment budget for adult undergrads
- **5 out of 8** schools secret-shopped failed to respond to inquiries within 48 hours
- **71.1%** view institutional websites as most successful recruitment tool

> *Source: [TargetX: 2019 AdultStudentsTALK Survey](https://www.targetx.com/blog/2019-adult-studentstalk-survey-main-themes-and-key-takeaways/)*

### HigherEdTALK
Survey of **493 senior team members** at U.S. colleges and universities:
- **70%** said competition for prospective students was the #1 external challenge
- **55%** cited public uncertainty about the value of higher education
- **54%** said growing enrollment was their top strategic priority

> *Source: [Stamats News: HigherEdTALK Study](https://www.stamats.com/news/stamats-new-higheredtalk-study-reveals-concerns-senior-team-members-colleges-universities)*
