CATEGORY 1: ACCESSIBILITY (ADA / WCAG 2.2 COMPLIANCE)
================================================================================

[CRITICAL] No alt text on ANY image
  - Header logo, footer logo, and all 6 menu images have zero alt text
  - Violates WCAG 2.1 Level A (Success Criterion 1.1.1)
  - Screen readers will announce these as unlabeled images or skip them
  - Potential legal liability under ADA Title III

[CRITICAL] No ARIA labels on navigation menus
  - Expandable dropdown menus lack aria-expanded, aria-haspopup attributes
  - Nested submenus have no aria-controls or aria-owns relationships
  - Keyboard-only users cannot determine menu state (open/closed)

[CRITICAL] Search field has no associated <label> element
  - "Search This Site" appears to be placeholder text, not a proper label
  - Violates WCAG 2.1 Level A (Success Criterion 1.3.1)

[CRITICAL] Symbol legend (^ * **) is inaccessible
  - Screen readers will read "African American Studies caret" not
    "African American Studies, also available as a minor"
  - No aria-describedby or title attributes to explain symbols
  - Legend is buried at bottom of a long list - sighted users may miss it too

[HIGH] No visible focus indicators documented
  - Keyboard users cannot see which element is currently focused
  - Violates WCAG 2.1 Level AA (Success Criterion 2.4.7)

[HIGH] Missing semantic landmark regions
  - No <nav> element wrapping navigation (uses generic <ul>)
  - No <main> element wrapping primary content (only a skip-link target ID)
  - No <footer> semantic element for footer region
  - No <article> or <section> elements organizing major groupings
  - Screen reader users cannot jump between page regions

[HIGH] No lang="en" attribute confirmed on <html> tag
  - Screen readers may not select correct pronunciation engine
  - Violates WCAG 2.1 Level A (Success Criterion 3.1.1)

[MEDIUM] No skip-to-sections links
  - Skip link exists for #main-content but with 80+ items, users need
    skip links to "Minors" and "Pre-Professional Programs" sections too

[MEDIUM] No high-contrast mode or text resize controls
  - Many higher-ed sites now offer built-in accessibility toolbars

[LOW] No accessibility statement page linked from this page
  - Best practice: link to a page describing conformance level and contact

CATEGORY 2: SEO & TECHNICAL MARKUP
================================================================================

[CRITICAL] No meta description tag
  - Google will auto-generate a snippet from page content (unpredictable)
  - Competitors with crafted descriptions will get higher CTR in search

[CRITICAL] No Open Graph (og:) meta tags
  - When shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, or messaging apps:
    * No title, description, or image will render
    * Link preview will be blank or auto-scraped poorly
  - Missing: og:title, og:description, og:image, og:url, og:type

[CRITICAL] No Twitter/X Card meta tags
  - Same social sharing problem as Open Graph
  - Missing: twitter:card, twitter:title, twitter:description, twitter:image

[CRITICAL] No Schema.org structured data (JSON-LD)
  - Google cannot display rich results for this page
  - Missing schemas that should be present:
    * CollegeOrUniversity (institution)
    * EducationalOrganization
    * Course or ProgramOfStudy (for each major)
    * BreadcrumbList (breadcrumbs exist visually but not in markup)
    * ItemList (for the list of majors)
  - Competitors with structured data get rich snippets in search results

[CRITICAL] No canonical URL tag
  - Risk of duplicate content if page is accessible at multiple URLs
  - Google may index the wrong version

[HIGH] Legacy Google Analytics (UA-3745152-31)
  - Universal Analytics was sunset by Google in July 2023
  - Should be using Google Analytics 4 (GA4) exclusively
  - UA properties are no longer processing data

[HIGH] Heading hierarchy issues
  - Page has H1 then multiple H2s but no H3s for sub-groupings
  - Business specializations under a flat H2 instead of nested H3s
  - Screen readers and SEO crawlers use heading hierarchy to understand
    content structure

[MEDIUM] No sitemap.xml or robots.txt reference in HTML
  - Should have <link rel="sitemap"> in <head>

[MEDIUM] No hreflang tags
  - Google Translate widget is present but no hreflang tells search
    engines about language availability

[MEDIUM] Images have no loading="lazy" attribute
  - All images load immediately, slowing initial page render

[LOW] No favicon/apple-touch-icon references visible
  - Bookmarks and browser tabs may show generic icons

CATEGORY 3: CONTENT GAPS (WHAT'S NOT ON THE PAGE)
================================================================================

[CRITICAL] Zero program descriptions
  - Every major is just a name and a link. No blurb, no sentence, nothing.
  - A student scanning 60+ majors has no way to understand what each
    program covers without clicking every single link
  - Peer institutions provide at minimum a 1-2 sentence description

[CRITICAL] No student outcomes data
  - Missing for every program:
    * Employment rate after graduation
    * Average starting salary
    * Graduate school placement rate
    * Employers who hire Coe grads
    * Career paths associated with each major
  - This is the #1 thing Gen Z students and parents look for (per
    multiple higher-ed surveys 2023-2025)

[CRITICAL] No accreditation information displayed
  - Page shows no badges or mentions of:
    * Regional accreditor (HLC - Higher Learning Commission)
    * Program-specific accreditations (AACSB for business, CCNE for
      nursing, ABET for engineering, NASM for music, etc.)
  - Accreditation is a top trust signal for tuition-paying families

[HIGH] No faculty information
  - No faculty names, credentials, research interests, or student-faculty
    ratio per department
  - Students want to know who they'll learn from

[HIGH] No degree requirements summary
  - No credit hours, course counts, or time-to-completion per major
  - No sample 4-year plans or course roadmaps
  - Students can't estimate workload or plan ahead from this page

[HIGH] No student testimonials or success stories
  - No quotes, no alumni spotlights, no "day in the life" features
  - Zero social proof for any program

[HIGH] No video content whatsoever
  - No department overview videos
  - No student spotlights
  - No virtual classroom tours
  - No professor introductions
  - Video is the dominant content format for Gen Z decision-making

[HIGH] No explanation for discontinued programs
  - 5 majors and 3 minors are marked "no longer accepting students"
  - No explanation of whether this is temporary, permanent, or
    transitional (teach-out)
  - Current students in those programs get no guidance from this page

[MEDIUM] No tuition or cost information
  - No per-program cost estimates
  - No link to net price calculator from this page
  - No ROI data (cost vs. expected earnings)

[MEDIUM] No admission requirements by program
  - No GPA expectations, prerequisites, audition requirements (music/
    theatre), portfolio requirements (art), or test score expectations

[MEDIUM] No research opportunities mentioned
  - Liberal arts colleges emphasize undergraduate research, but this
    page doesn't mention it for any program

[MEDIUM] No internship or experiential learning information
  - No co-op programs, clinical placements, student teaching details,
    or field experience descriptions

[MEDIUM] No dual-degree or combined program guidance
  - Students interested in double majors or major/minor combos get
    no guidance

[LOW] No "NEW" or "FEATURED" labels
  - Data Science and Neuroscience are relatively new programs that
    could attract attention if highlighted

[LOW] No program comparison chart
  - No side-by-side view of related majors (e.g., all 8 business tracks)

CATEGORY 4: USER EXPERIENCE (UX) & INTERFACE DESIGN
================================================================================

[CRITICAL] No search or filter functionality
  - 82 programs listed on one page with no way to:
    * Search by keyword
    * Filter by category (STEM, Business, Arts, etc.)
    * Filter by degree type (major, minor, pre-professional)
    * Filter by availability (hide discontinued programs)
    * Filter by "also available as minor"
  - User must scroll through entire alphabetical list
  - This is the single biggest UX failure on the page

[HIGH] No category/department grouping view
  - All 60 majors are in one flat alphabetical list
  - No toggle between "Alphabetical" and "By Department" views
  - Students interested in "something in science" must scan every entry

[HIGH] Confusing symbol notation system
  - ^ for "also a minor", * for "closed major", ** for "closed minor"
  - Legend is at the BOTTOM of the list, not the top
  - Users encounter symbols before encountering their meaning
  - No tooltip or hover explanation on each symbol
  - Visually indistinguishable at a glance (small superscript characters)

[HIGH] No mobile-optimized program cards
  - Just a text list of links - no visual hierarchy
  - Modern standard: card-based layout with icon, title, brief
    description, and CTA per program

[HIGH] Discontinued programs mixed with active programs
  - "Environmental Science*" sits right next to "Environmental Studies"
  - Misleading for students who click expecting to enroll
  - Should be separated or clearly visually distinguished (greyed out,
    separate section, or hidden by default with "show discontinued" toggle)

[MEDIUM] No "undecided student" pathway
  - Page assumes students already know what they want
  - No "Explore Your Interests" quiz or guided pathway
  - No "Most Popular Majors" or "Fastest Growing Programs" callout
  - No "Don't know what to study?" help section

[MEDIUM] Redundant/confusing entries
  - "Molecular Biology" and "Molecular Biology - Biology" listed separately
  - "Spanish" and "Spanish Studies" listed separately with no distinction
  - "Theatre Arts", "Performing Arts - Theatre Arts", and "Theatre Arts
    Management - Theatre Arts" are three separate entries
  - Users cannot tell the difference without clicking each one

[MEDIUM] Page is extremely long with no anchoring
  - No sticky table of contents
  - No "back to top" button
  - No anchor links from a top-of-page overview
  - On mobile, this is a very long scroll

[LOW] No dark mode support
  - Increasingly expected in 2025-2026, especially by younger users

[LOW] No print-friendly stylesheet
  - Parents and counselors often print program lists

CATEGORY 7: PRIVACY, COMPLIANCE & LEGAL
================================================================================

[CRITICAL] No cookie consent banner
  - Page loads Hotjar, Google Analytics, Google Ads, Google Tag Manager,
    and Mautic tracking - ALL before any user consent
  - Violates GDPR (if any EU visitors), ePrivacy Directive, and
    increasingly U.S. state privacy laws (CCPA, CPRA, VCDPA, CPA, etc.)
  - Hotjar specifically records session replays - extremely high privacy
    sensitivity without consent

[HIGH] No WCAG conformance claim
  - No accessibility statement linked or displayed
  - No stated target level (A, AA, AAA)
  - Best practice: publish an accessibility page with contact for
    accommodation requests

[HIGH] No privacy policy link on page body
  - Privacy policy exists in footer but is not contextually linked near
    tracking-heavy areas (forms, request info CTAs)

[MEDIUM] No FERPA disclaimer near any student data references
  - If outcome data were added, FERPA disclaimers would be needed

[MEDIUM] No data retention disclosure
  - Mautic marketing automation is collecting behavioral data
  - No disclosure about how long visitor data is stored

[LOW] No cookie preference center
  - Even if a consent banner were added, users should be able to
    granularly control which cookies they accept

CATEGORY 8: PERFORMANCE & TECHNICAL DEBT
================================================================================

[HIGH] Multiple synchronous tracking scripts
  - Hotjar, GTM, GA, Google Ads, and Mautic all load in <head>
  - These are render-blocking and slow initial page load
  - Should use async/defer attributes or load via GTM exclusively

[HIGH] Legacy Universal Analytics (UA) still running
  - UA-3745152-31 is a Universal Analytics property
  - Google stopped processing UA data in July 2023
  - This is dead code that still slows the page

[MEDIUM] No image lazy loading
  - All images load eagerly even if below the fold
  - Should use loading="lazy" on non-critical images

[MEDIUM] No Content Security Policy (CSP) headers evident
  - Multiple third-party scripts without CSP increases XSS risk

[MEDIUM] Concrete5 CMS (legacy)
  - While ConcreteCMS is still maintained, it has a much smaller
    ecosystem than WordPress, Drupal, or modern headless CMS options
  - May limit available plugins for features listed above

[LOW] Google Translate widget is inline JavaScript
  - Legacy implementation; modern approach would use server-side
    translation or a more performant widget

[LOW] No service worker or PWA capability
  - Page cannot work offline or be "installed" as an app

CATEGORY 10: CONTENT INCONSISTENCIES & NAMING PROBLEMS
================================================================================

[HIGH] Duplicate/ambiguous program listings
  - "Molecular Biology" (standalone, closed) AND "Molecular Biology -
    Biology" (active) - confusing
  - "Spanish" AND "Spanish Studies" - both link to /foreign-language,
    what's the difference?
  - "Environmental Science" (closed) AND "Environmental and Ecological
    Science - Biology" AND "Environmental Studies" - three similar names

[HIGH] Inconsistent naming conventions
  - Some majors use "Department - Specialization" format:
    "Finance - Business Administration"
  - Others use "Specialization - Department" format:
    "Media Production - Art"
  - Others have no department suffix at all: "Accounting" (which IS
    under Business Administration)
  - No consistent pattern for students to understand structure

[MEDIUM] "Pre-Music Therapy" links to Music page
  - Every other pre-professional program has its own dedicated page
  - Pre-Music Therapy links to the general Music department page
  - Inconsistent experience

[MEDIUM] "Interdisciplinary Science" links to /general-science
  - URL slug doesn't match the program name
  - May confuse users and hurt SEO

[LOW] Page title redundancy
  - Title: "Majors & Areas of Study - On Campus | Coe College"
  - The "On Campus" qualifier implies there's an online version, but
    this distinction isn't made clear on the page itself until a small
    link at the bottom
